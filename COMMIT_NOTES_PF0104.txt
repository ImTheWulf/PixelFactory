PF-0104 fix Repair Toolbox overflow — actually verified this time in a real browser

Changed:
- PF-0103 fixed the generic .repair-tool-inline rule but missed a THIRD
  layer: Alpha, Orphan, Edge, Morphology, and Jaggy Cleanup's two-control
  rows carry an extra `.repair-two-control-row` class with its own
  `!important` override (`minmax(220px,1fr) minmax(170px,240px)`, ~390px
  minimum) that sits later in the stylesheet than the PF-0103 fix, so it
  won regardless. That's why those 5 panes were still cut off after
  PF-0103 even though Resize/Palette/Palette Lock/Pixel Snap were fine.
- Changed .repair-two-control-row to `repeat(2, minmax(0, 1fr)) !important`
  — same flexible pattern as everything else now uses.

How this was actually verified (not just reasoned about):
- Rendered the real index.html + styles.css + JS in a headless Chromium
  (Playwright), clicked into Palette Lab, clicked through all 9 Repair
  Toolbox tabs, and measured actual DOM bounding boxes against the
  container's right edge — not a visual guess.
- Before this fix: orphans/alpha/edge/morphology/jaggy all measured a real
  32px overflow on the visible toggle button. snap/lock/palette/resize
  measured zero.
- After this fix: all 9 tabs measure zero overflow at the viewport width
  your screenshots were taken at (tested 2500px and 1920px). At narrower
  widths (1300-1440px) there's a ~2-4px sub-pixel rounding artifact from
  the grid algorithm dividing available space — confirmed via screenshot
  that it does NOT visibly clip any text or button, well within the
  border-radius tolerance. Not chasing that further unless it turns out to
  matter on your actual monitor.

Test:
- Hard refresh (cache-bust bumped again).
- Click through all 9 Repair Toolbox tabs. Every toggle button, slider,
  and input should be fully visible with no clipping.
